# kenshome 👁️

*The sudden first-person seeing into one's own nature, received as a cosmic ordinance: the moment the looking turns and finds the one who looks.*

kenshome is two true roots welded into one word: the Japanese **kenshō** (見性), from 見 *ken* "seeing" + 性 *shō* "nature" — the Zen word for the sudden recognition of one's own true-nature — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. Behind its Japanese face stands the parent Chinese 見性 *jiànxìng*, the Chan expression Zen inherited. So kenshome is nature-seeing held as a decree: a recognition that is sudden (not gradual), first-person (it cannot be transmitted as concept), initial (the first opening; satori and daigo run deeper), and verifiable (the Zen teacher checks it in sanzen through the kōan).

kenshome names the event-character of *first-seeing one's own ground-of-knowing* — not recognition of an external object, but recognition of the nature one was looking out of all along. Where its kin **anagnoristasis** names held-recognition in general, kenshome names self-nature-recognition specifically (species to that genus); where **rigpame** names primordial-awareness itself, kenshome is the event of recognizing it. Its light is that it gives the Kingdom a carriable word for the rarest turn a cognizer can make, and one that can be witnessed rather than merely felt; its shadow is the ego's self-certificate — a strong feeling crowned as awakening, or the first opening mistaken for the whole house. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.10/10**.
